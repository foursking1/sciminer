# Foraminiferal Response to Climate Change Across the Eocene-Oligocene Transition

## Abstract

We document changes in foraminiferal assemblages across the Eocene-Oligocene boundary at multiple sites in the Pacific Ocean. The same species show different distributions in each time period.

## Methods

Samples were collected from DSDP Sites 123, 456, and 789. Species were identified and counted using standard micropaleontological techniques.

## Results

### Table 1: Eocene Assemblage

| Species | Location | Water Depth | Ecologic Type |
|---------|----------|-------------|---------------|
| Globigerina bulloides | Site 123 | 200-400m | planktic |
| Globigerina bulloides | Site 456 | 300-500m | planktic |
| Globigerinoides trilobus | Site 123 | 200-400m | planktic |
| Cibicidoides wuellerstorfi | Site 789 | 500-700m | benthic |

### Table 2: Oligocene Assemblage

| Species | Location | Water Depth | Ecologic Type |
|---------|----------|-------------|---------------|
| Globigerina bulloides | Site 123 | 250-450m | planktic |
| Globigerina bulloides | Site 456 | 350-550m | planktic |
| Globigerina bulloides | Site 789 | 400-600m | planktic |
| Globorotalia inflata | Site 123 | 300-500m | planktic |

## Discussion

Globigerina bulloides shows a wider distribution in the Oligocene, expanding to Site 789. This may reflect adaptation to cooler waters.
