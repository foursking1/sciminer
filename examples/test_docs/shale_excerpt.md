# Geochemical Characterization of Shale Gas Reservoirs

## Abstract

This study presents geochemical and petrophysical data from the Marcellus Shale in the Appalachian Basin. Samples were analyzed for total organic carbon (TOC), vitrinite reflectance (Ro), porosity, and mineral composition.

## Methods

Core samples were collected from wells in Pennsylvania and West Virginia. TOC was measured using a LECO carbon analyzer. Vitrinite reflectance was determined using standard petrographic methods. Porosity was measured by helium porosimetry.

## Discussion

The high TOC values (3.8-6.2%) indicate excellent source rock potential. Vitrinite reflectance values (1.8-2.2%) suggest the samples are in the dry gas window.
